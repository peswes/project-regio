Introduction

About 70% of all the edible vegetable oil consumed in Nigeria is made from refined palm kernel oil; hence edible oil production is a viable business in the country as well as globally due to high demands of its products by vegetable oil refiners, confectioners and cosmetics industry.

Nigeria with an estimated population of over 170 million people, a national population growth rate of 5.7% per annum and an average economic growth rate of 3.5% per annum in the past five years, has a large market for palm kernel oil and cake. Due to high demand and subsequent shortages observed in Nigeria, consecutive government in the past 15years made consistent policy aimed at stimulating the local production and attaining self sufficiency in edible oil production.

As a result of the viability of the business, sometimes buyers even pay in advance in order to secure an expected batch production. Prices of the main products i.e. PKO and PKC fluctuate up and down depending on the season of the year. However, the prices of the raw materials i.e. palm kernel nuts also fluctuate along with the price of PKO.

Apart from the high demand of palm kernel oil and other by-products of the extraction process locally, the demand for palm kernel oil globally is equally very high which gives the opportunity to export in the future once local demands is met.

In addition, palm kernel shells (PKS) which is considered as a waste material hence no real economical value in Nigeria because it is used as substitute for firewood has value in some foreign markets
